Energy Destination & Grid Export
Where does the sun go?

Generation is only half the battle; efficiency is the other. Our system prioritizes "Self-Consumption"—using your own solar energy to power your lights and charge your batteries before even thinking about the grid. This maximizes your ROI and keeps the local energy "behind the meter."

Chart 3: Breakdown of solar utilization. "Self-consumed" energy stays in the neighborhood, while "Exported" energy represents the surplus sold back to the utility grid.

The Duck Curve & Grid Stress
The Challenge: The Afternoon Ramp

As the sun sets, solar production drops just as families arrive home and turn on their appliances. This creates the famous "Duck Curve"—a massive spike in grid demand. Our smart Energy Management System (EMS) uses battery storage to "flatten" this curve, preventing grid blackouts and reducing high-cost energy imports during peak hours.

Chart 4: The "Belly" of the duck shows surplus solar at noon. The "Neck" shows the evening peak. Our goal is to keep the blue Net Load line as flat as possible.

Battery Storage Utilization
Storing Sunlight for the Night

Our batteries are the heart of the community. They charge silently during the quiet morning hours when the sun is at its peak and discharge strategically when the grid is most expensive. This is the "buffer" that makes energy independence possible.

Chart 5: Shows the average State of Charge (SoC) across the neighborhood. Notice how the batteries reach 100% capacity just before the sun starts to set.
